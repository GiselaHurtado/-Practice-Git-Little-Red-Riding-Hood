Little Red Riding Hood

Part 1: Little Red Riding

Once upon a time, in a small village on the edge of a forest, there lived a little girl known as Little Red Riding Hood. She was called this because she always wore a red cloak with a hood that her grandmother had made for her.

Part 2: A Task for Red Riding Hood

One sunny morning, Red Riding Hood’s mother said, “Your grandmother is feeling unwell. Please take her this basket of cakes and a little pot of butter.” Red Riding Hood was very happy to help and set off to her grandmother’s house.

Part 3: The Warning

Before she left, her mother warned her, “Go straight to Grandma’s house and don’t stray from the path. The woods can be very dangerous.”

Part 4: The Wolf

As Red Riding Hood walked through the woods, she met a wolf. The wolf wanted to eat her up but knew he couldn’t because some woodcutters were nearby. Instead, he asked her where she was going. Innocently, she told him about her sick grandmother’s house.

Part 5: The Deception

The wolf quickly ran ahead to Grandma’s house, where he ate the grandmother up, put on her clothes, and climbed into her bed, waiting for Red Riding Hood.

Part 6: The Arrival

When Red Riding Hood arrived, she found her ‘grandmother’ looking very strange. “What big eyes you have,” she said. “All the better to see you with, my dear,” replied the wolf.

Part 7: The Discovery

Red Riding Hood then said, “What big ears you have.” “All the better to hear you with, my dear,” said the wolf. Then she said, “What big teeth you have.” The wolf replied, “All the better to eat you with!” and jumped out of bed.

Part 8: The Rescue

Just as the wolf was about to pounce, a woodcutter who was passing by heard the commotion and rushed in. He saved Red Riding Hood and her grandmother by cutting the wolf open and pulling them out.

Part 9: The Happy Ending

Red Riding Hood learned to never trust strangers and to always listen to her mother. She, her mother, and her grandmother had tea with the cakes she had brought and lived happily ever after.
